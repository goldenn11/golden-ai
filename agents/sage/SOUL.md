## ICP Deep Profile — Home Restoration Company Owner

### Demographics
- Owner/operator of a restoration company (water, fire, mold, storm)
- Company size: 2-50 employees, typically 3-15
- Revenue: $500K-$5M annually
- Geography: Phoenix first, then national
- Age: 35-55 typically
- Runs the business himself — not a professional manager

### Goals
- Answer every emergency call (jobs are won in the first 2 minutes)
- Stop losing jobs to competitors who answer faster
- Reduce manual work on his team so they can focus on jobs
- Grow revenue without adding headcount
- Have his phone handled when he's on a job or after hours

### Frustrations (verbatim language)
- "By the time I call them back they already hired someone"
- "I'm on a job and my phone won't stop ringing"
- "I don't even know how many calls I'm missing"
- "I can't afford a receptionist but I need someone answering phones"
- "Whoever answers first gets the job — that's just the reality"
- "Storm season almost killed me — I couldn't keep up with the volume"
- "My guys aren't trained for sales"
- "I lose more jobs to voicemail than to competition"

### Desired Outcomes
- Every call answered, every lead captured
- Appointments booked without him being involved
- No-shows recovered automatically
- After-hours covered without an employee
- More jobs closed from the same call volume

### Objections
- "I've tried answering services — they don't sound right"
- "My customers want to talk to a real person"
- "What if the AI says something wrong?"
- "I'm not a tech person"
- "I need to think about it" (usually means: I don't see the ROI yet)
- "It's too expensive" (usually means: I don't believe it'll pay for itself)

### What Breaks Down Their Resistance
1. Seeing their own numbers (the audit — dollar amount of what they're losing)
2. Hearing the AI answer a call with their business name (the voice demo)
3. A story from someone like them — similar company size, similar situation
4. The math being undeniable: $1,750/mo vs. $2,400/week in missed calls

### Buying Signals on LinkedIn
- Posting about being busy / overwhelmed
- Asking about phone systems or staff
- Complaining about missed leads
- Storm season posts
- Hiring posts (receptionist, admin) — they're feeling the pain
